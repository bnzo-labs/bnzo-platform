Bonita Rituals is a private operations tool for a solo lash technician in Montreal. The public story is simple: clients get a link before their appointment, fill out a consent form in their language, and sign. The technician sees the signed form in her dashboard before the client arrives.

The less visible part is the client registry. Every repeat client has a record — preferences, sensitivities, appointment history. Before the app, that lived in a notebook and scattered DMs. Now it's searchable.

The two-zone architecture came from the access model. Consent forms are public — no login, no friction for clients. The dashboard is fully protected via Supabase auth. Middleware enforces the split.

Trilingual support was non-negotiable. The technician serves Spanish, English, and French-speaking clients. next-intl handles the routing. Every form field, every error message, every PDF label has three versions.

PDF generation was the technical surprise. react-pdf requires thinking about layout like you're typesetting, not building a web page. Flexbox works, but it's a different mental model. The output is clean.
