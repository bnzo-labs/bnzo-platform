Cook For Friends is a home kitchen business in Montreal. The owner, Carolina, cooks for private clients, runs courses, and manages a growing roster through word of mouth. The operational overhead was all manual — orders tracked in WhatsApp, clients in a spreadsheet, revenue in her head.

The dashboard covers the full operational surface: orders, clients, courses, and finances. Each section has a table with real data, not mockups. KPIs on the home screen give a quick view of the month.

The AI agent (Muffi) lives in the lower-right corner — a floating chat interface that answers operational questions without requiring Carolina to navigate to a specific page. "How much did I make last month?" is faster than opening the finances tab.

Social media management is the newest addition. The dashboard has a three-tab social section: scheduled posts, brand guidelines, and analytics. Posts are managed with optimistic updates — changes appear immediately, roll back cleanly on failure.

The next phase replaces Muffi (OpenAI) with Genaro, the bnzo-agency agent with full business context. That upgrade makes the agent actually aware of Carolina's clients, orders, and preferences — not just a generic assistant.
