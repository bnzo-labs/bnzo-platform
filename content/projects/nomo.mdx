Nomo is a Montreal-specific parking sign scanner. Point the camera at a sign, get a plain-language verdict: can you park, for how long, and when does the next restriction kick in.

The core pipeline is camera → OpenAI Vision → structured JSON verdict → rendered result screen. The AI reads the sign so you don't have to decode Montreal's layered restriction system. Signs with time-of-day rules, seasonal restrictions, and borough-specific exceptions are all handled by the model.

The notification loop is the killer feature. Nomo knows when your time is up. It sends a reminder before the restriction kicks in so you can move the car or top up. On iOS, this required working around aggressive background process termination — reminders are scheduled as push notifications at scan time, not managed by a background process.

MVP is at ~35%. The scan history and home screen are working. Auth broke during setup (Google/Apple OAuth not completing the callback), and i18n language switching needs a fix pass before the next wave.

Wave 3 completes the core pipeline: OpenAI Vision integration, scan result screen polish, and address search. Bilingual FR/EN from launch — Montreal is bilingual, so the app is too.
